The Impact of Illegal Dumping

Environmental Damage

Illegal dumping contaminates soil and water sources, harms wildlife, and contributes to climate change through methane emissions from decomposing waste.

40% of freshwater pollution comes from illegal dumping

Public Health Risks

Dumped waste attracts pests, creates breeding grounds for mosquitoes, and can expose communities to hazardous materials and toxic substances.

28% increase in disease vectors near dump sites

Economic Costs

Taxpayers bear the burden of cleanup costs, while property values decline in areas affected by illegal dumping.

$11B annual cleanup costs nationwide

Community Impact

Illegal dumping creates eyesores, reduces quality of life, and disproportionately affects disadvantaged neighborhoods.

3x more likely in low-income areas

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about illegal dumping and reporting.

What constitutes illegal dumping?

Illegal dumping is the disposal of waste on public or private property without proper authorization. This includes household trash, construction debris, appliances, hazardous materials, and any other waste not disposed of through proper channels.

 What information should I include in my report?

Please provide as much detail as possible: location (address or GPS coordinates), description of the waste, date/time observed, photos if safe to take, and any information about vehicles or individuals involved.

 Is my report anonymous?

Yes, all reports can be submitted anonymously. However, providing contact information allows us to follow up if we need more details and to update you on the status of your report.

 What happens after I submit a report?

Reports are reviewed and forwarded to the appropriate local authorities for investigation. Cleanup timelines vary based on the severity and location of the dumping.

 How can I properly dispose of large items?

Most municipalities offer bulk item pickup or have designated drop-off locations. Hazardous materials should be taken to special collection facilities. Check with your local waste management department for specific guidelines.
